# Medication Guide

## Aspirin

### Description
Aspirin (acetylsalicylic acid) is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID).

### Uses
- Pain relief
- Fever reduction
- Anti-inflammatory
- Blood thinning (cardiovascular protection)

### Dosage
- Adults: 325-650mg every 4-6 hours as needed
- Children under 12: Consult physician (risk of Reye's syndrome)
- Maximum daily dose: 4000mg

### Side Effects
Common side effects include:
- Stomach upset
- Heartburn
- Nausea

Serious side effects:
- Gastrointestinal bleeding
- Allergic reactions
- Ringing in ears (tinnitus)

### Contraindications
Do NOT use if:
- Allergic to aspirin or NSAIDs
- Active bleeding disorder
- Severe liver or kidney disease
- Children with viral infections (chicken pox, flu)
